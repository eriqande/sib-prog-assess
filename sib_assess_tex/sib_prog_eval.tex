\section*{Appendix 4 - AA's take} 

Given data of sufficient quality ($\geq 10$ alleles and $\geq 15$ loci) PRT can be generally 
recommended for all models except those dominated by small sibling groups. Interestingly, given it's 
emphasis on large sibling groups, PRT consistently performs as well, or better, than all other 
algorithms for the NoSibs, AllHalf and AllPatHalf models, across all error models. 

For larger sibling groups the situation varies (we again consider $\geq 10$ alleles and $\geq 15$ 
loci). For the OneBig and OneBig\_H models PRT appears to be consistently most accurate, although the 
accuracy of CO and FF is comparable. For the BigSGs and BigSGs\_H models CO offers the best accuracy 
consistently, although CP, PRT and FF remain viable alternatives. For these two models, memory 
requirements sometimes hampered the performance of PRT. For especially large problems careful tuning of 
memory parameters will be required. 

For the SmallSGs and  SmallSGs\_H model CO and CP was consistently most accurate.
